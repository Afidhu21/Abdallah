ABOUT TESLA COMPANY

Absolutely! Here are several true and remarkable stories about Tesla and its journey:

1. Tesla Secret Master Plan (2006) Elon Musk published a blog post in 2006 titled “The Secret Tesla Motors Master Plan (just between you and me)”.
The plan was simple but visionary: Build a sports car (Roadster)
Use that money to build an affordable car (Model S) Use that money to build an even more affordable car (Model 3)
Provide zero-emission electric power generation options (like solar)


2. The Model S Door Handle Problem Tesla Model S had a sleek, futuristic design with retractable door handles.
But these handles caused serious production problems in 2012. They would often malfunction,>
delaying shipments and angering customers. Elon Musk personally got involved, forcing the engineering team to
redesign the mechanism. His perfectionism was intense—he once demanded a complete redesign over a slight noise in the handle.


3. The Model 3 “Production Hell” In 2017 2018, Tesla faced what Elon Musk called “production hell” while ramping up Model 3 production.
The company bet everything on automation and built a highly robotic assembly line. But the robots failed to deliver,
causing delays and quality issues. Musk admitted later: “Excessive automation at Tesla was a mistake. To be precise, my mistake.”
To meet deadlines, Musk slept on the factory floor and turned an empty tent into an emergency production line.
It worked—Model 3 became a massive success.


4. Short Sellers and the “Funding Secured” Tweet Tesla became one of the most shorted stocks in history, with many investors betting the company would fail.
In 2018, Musk tweeted: “Am considering taking Tesla private at $420. Funding secured.”
The tweet caused chaos on Wall Street. The SEC sued Musk for misleading investors,
leading to a $40 million settlement and his removal as Tesla’s chairman for three years.
Still, Tesla’s stock later skyrocketed.


5. Tesla's Gigafactories: Built in Record Time Tesla began building massive “Gigafactories” to produce batteries and EVs around the world.
One of the most impressive was Gigafactory Shanghai, which went from an empty field to production
in less than 12 months in 2019—something unheard of in the auto industry.


6. Cybertruck Unbreakable Glass Fail (2019) At the Cybertruck launch event, Tesla chief designer threw a metal ball at the truck “armor
glass” to show how strong it was.To everyone's shock, it cracked badly.
Elon Musk was visibly surprised but laughed it off.
Despite the fail, the Cybertruck received hundreds of thousands of preorders.
